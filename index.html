On-device CoreML

Milely learns from you. On-device.

The third time you classify a trip from your office to Acme Realty's listing as "showing," Milely starts pre-filling that purpose for you.

The model — Apple's NLEmbedding stacked with a CreateML logistic-regression classifier — trains locally on your device against your classifications. No Milely account. No Milely server. There can't be a leak because the training data never leaves your phone.

Built for the way you actually work.

You don't classify trips at your desk. You classify them at a stoplight, between showings, on the way to the next job. Milely's smaller features are built for that.

Auto-classify presets

Tap "Always business at 1428 Maple St" or "Always personal" — Milely creates a rule. Future trips through that address auto-pre-fill.

Favorite trips, one tap

Save common drives as templates — Office to listing, Home to job site, Costco run. Surface as Quick Start on the Dashboard.

Sunday review reminder

Sunday 6 PM, if you have unclassified trips, Milely pings you. If everything's classified, it stays silent. Five minutes a week.

"I forgot for three weeks."

Calendar-based recovery reads recent events with locations — on-device, never transmitted — and proposes them as trips.

Project & client tagging

Within a Business, tag each trip with a project — "1428 Maple," "Smith matter." Surfaces on the CSV and the PDF.

Receipt OCR via Vision

Snap a parking stub or toll receipt. On-device Vision OCR pulls the total — no cloud, no third-party service.

Backups your way — your data, your cloud.

Milely backs up to iCloud Drive, Google Drive, Dropbox, or OneDrive — whichever folder you point it at via the iOS Files picker. The backup is the user's data going to the user's storage. Milely doesn't see it, doesn't proxy it, doesn't index it. Manual JSON export is also free and always available, no subscription needed.
