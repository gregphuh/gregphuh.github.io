Watch your tax savings tick up as you drive

Set your federal bracket once. Then every mile shows up as a real dollar value in real time — not just a deduction line, but the actual tax saved at your bracket. The Dashboard ticker turns "I drove 14 miles" into "I just saved $3.24."

Tap. Drive. Save.

Tap Start, drive, tap End. Milely captures your route on a live map, calculates the miles, and asks you to classify the trip when you're done.

Or fully automatic

Opt into auto-detect and Milely uses CoreMotion + GPS to recognize when you start driving — no manual taps, no missed trips.

Receipt OCR via Vision

Snap a parking stub or toll receipt. On-device Vision OCR pulls the total and attaches it to the trip — no cloud upload, no third-party service.

Milely learns from you — on your phone, never on our servers.

The third time you classify a trip from your office to Acme Realty's listing as "showing," Milely starts pre-filling that purpose for you. The model that does it — Apple's NLEmbedding stacked with a CreateML logistic-regression classifier — is trained locally on your device, against your classification history. No data ever leaves your phone. There's no Milely account. There's no Milely server. There can't be a leak of training data because the training data never goes anywhere.

That's the whole privacy story in one paragraph: your driving habits are not the product. We sell software, not signals.

Audit-defensive by design.

If the IRS ever wants to see your records, Milely's job is to make your life boring.

Audit Lock

January 31 each year, last year's records lock automatically. Unlocking is logged with a timestamp. Your CPA stops worrying about mid-audit edits — and if you do need to amend a return, there's a clean paper trail showing exactly what changed and when.

Per-trip edit history

Every field-level change to a saved trip — miles, purpose, business, vehicle, anything — records a TripEditEvent with timestamp plus old and new value. Tap any trip and the "Edit history" section shows the full audit trail. CPA-defensive, IRS-defensive, ex-spouse-defensive.

Year-end odometer reconciliation

IRS Form 2106 and Schedule C want beginning- and end-of-year odometer readings per vehicle. Milely captures them, prints them on the annual PDF, and reconciles them against the miles you actually logged. No more guessing in April.

Built for the way you actually work.

You don't classify trips at your desk. You classify them at a stoplight, between showings, on the way to the next job. Milely is built for that.

Auto-classify smart presets

Tap a trip and hit "Always business at 1428 Maple St" or "Always personal" — Milely creates a rule. Future trips through that address auto-pre-fill so you never have to think about them again.

Favorite trips, one tap

Save your common drives as templates — "Office to listing," "Home to job site," "Costco run." They surface as a Quick Start row on the Dashboard. One tap, you're recording, pre-classified.

Sunday review reminder

Sunday at 6 PM, if you have unclassified trips, Milely pings you with a home-screen badge and a quiet notification. If everything's already classified, it stays silent. Five-minute weekly habit, year-end stress gone.

"I forgot to use the app for three weeks."

It happens. Milely's calendar-based recovery reads your recent calendar events with locations — on-device, never transmitted — and proposes them as trips you can log retroactively. Each one becomes a normal audit-trailed entry.

Project & client tagging

Within a Business, tag each trip with a project or client — "1428 Maple listing," "Dallas job," "Smith matter." Free-text per trip, surfaces on the CSV and the PDF report. Real-estate agents, contractors, and consultants all asked for this; it's there.

Live tax ticker

Dashboard card shows year-to-date deduction and estimated tax savings at your bracket, ticking up as you drive. The "I just saved $3.24" effect is annoyingly motivating.

Export wherever your accountant wants it.

One Export button on the Reports tab opens a menu with three formats — pick whichever your CPA, your bookkeeper, or your spreadsheet brain prefers:

  • PDF — IRS Pub. 463-formatted trip log with cover page, by-business breakdown, and per-trip details. Hand it to your CPA.
  • Excel (.xls) — opens in Excel, Numbers, or Google Sheets with formatting intact. For when your bookkeeper says "send me a spreadsheet."
  • CSV — full Milely export plus a QuickBooks Online drop-in import variant. Mileage straight into QBO with no manual entry.

Every export uses the same source of truth: the trip data on your phone. No "sync first, then export" dance.

Backups your way — your data, your cloud.

Milely backs up to iCloud Drive, Google Drive, Dropbox, or OneDrive — whichever folder you point it at via the iOS Files picker. The backup is the user's data going to the user's storage. Milely doesn't see it, doesn't proxy it, doesn't index it. Manual JSON export is also free and always available, no subscription needed.
